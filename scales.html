Scales

Sat 20 November 2021

https://tones.wolfram.com/

At WolframTones, they have an amazing list of scales. I noticed they did not have a few scales so I have copied the list here so I can add/change them and add personal comments.

This is something I have wanted to explore for a very long time. I found old worksheets where I would write any new scale that I found. To me, scales are like punch cards where you remove certain things, it represents something different. In school, the lessons essentially went from harmonic structure to random. I have worked on finding chord structures using the scale as the "rule-of-law" to find their harmonic possibilities.

All scales start on note C. From there you can calculate the steps between notes to transpose them to the key you want. Some patterns could repeat. (Example: Ionian & Major)
